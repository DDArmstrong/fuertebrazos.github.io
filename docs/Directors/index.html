Article Pitch

BFFs of the power elite: The John Malone Mafia

I was curious about the demographics and relationships among directors on corporate boards. I obtained a list of companies in the Russell 3,000 - the top 3,000 US public companies by capitalization - and discovered that Reuters maintains company pages listing each director, their age, position, and when they were appointed to the board.

I built a scraper and went to work. The result was a dataset of about 45,000 names - and quite a few surprises:

The youngest public company CEO in America, who started his company at 19 and now runs a vaping empire.

A 99-year-old CEO, WWII airman, Harvard degree in math and physics, son of a Lebanese coal miner.

By ranking names by how often they appeared, I compiled a group of men who built their careers at one company - Liberty Media - and went on to serve on many other boards.

I call this group of board members the John Malone Mafia because they were all mentored by the cable media entrepreneur John Malone.

Methodology:

Methodology

Ticker list from ftserussell.com.

Board members from the “company people” pages at https://www.reuters.com.

Scraper built with Python’s BeautifulSoup library to pull board members from each company’s Reuters page.

Data analyzed using Pandas.

Github_repo: The code is available at https://github.com/fuertebrazos/directors
