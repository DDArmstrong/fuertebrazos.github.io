Article Pitch

The Upper Upper West Side Map of the Stars

A neighbor wrote a detailed historical account of all the famous people who formerly or currently live in my neighborhood, which runs from 96th Street to 125th Street in Manhattan. It encompasses Columbia University, borders on Harlem, and is sometimes called the “Upper Upper West Side.” He shared with me his spreadsheet of famous figures in our neighborhood from pre-Revolutionary times to the present.

methodology:

Methodology

I used Geocodio to get the latitude and longitude of the addresses, then dropped them into QGIS and combined them with Census shapefiles.

I wrote a Python program to filter the dataset down to people who are sufficiently well-known to have Wikipedia pages.

Because QGIS is limited in its ability to utilize drop-down filters, I re-created the map in Tableau, categorized the names by occupation, and gave users the ability to filter residents by the source of their fame.
